This paper proposes a new framework that leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) and a multi-agent architecture for quantitative stock investment and portfolio management. The framework integrates LLMs to generate diverse alpha factors and dynamically assess market conditions to address the instability issues in existing financial trading models. By extracting predictive signals from multimodal financial data, constructing a pool of trading agents with different risk preferences, and dynamically allocating weights based on real-time market conditions, this framework adapts to market changes and optimizes trading strategies. Extensive experiments on the Chinese stock market demonstrate that the framework significantly outperforms existing benchmarks across several financial metrics.

# Problem Statement

This chapter details a comprehensive framework for generating alpha factors and strategies in quantitative trading. The core of the framework is how to calculate alpha factors for multiple stocks over several trading periods and select the best-performing seed alpha based on backtesting results and current market conditions. The framework uses LLMs and multi-agent systems to dynamically mine and select seed alphas, ensuring adaptability to market changes. Seed alphas are expressed as mathematical formulas, combining cross-sectional and time-series operators, and include a process for refining and updating these alphas over time, ensuring their relevance to evolving market conditions.

## Alpha Factors and Strategies

Assume there are several stocks for the next trading period. For each period, multiple categories of alpha values can be calculated for each stock. These alpha values represent the stock’s potential value, and the best-performing seed alpha from each category is selected as the basis for the final strategy. The overall Alpha strategy is composed of the seed alphas selected from each category.

# Methodology

This chapter elaborates on the comprehensive framework for constructing and optimizing alpha strategies in quantitative trading. The framework consists of three key components: the Seed Alpha Factory, multi-agent decision-making processes, and a weight optimization method. The Seed Alpha Factory utilizes LLMs to filter and classify multimodal data, creating a robust set of seed alphas. The multi-agent system evaluates and selects these alphas based on different risk preferences and market conditions, ensuring reliability through a confidence scoring mechanism. Finally, deep neural networks (DNNs) are employed to optimize the weights of the selected alphas, predicting future returns and forming the foundation for dynamic and adaptive investment strategies.

## Framework Overview

The framework is divided into three main parts: the Seed Alpha Factory, multi-agent decision-making, and weight optimization methods. In the initial stage, LLMs are used to filter and classify multimodal documents, building the Seed Alpha Factory. The LLM’s ability to process large and diverse datasets ensures that the seed alpha set is comprehensive and robust, categorized according to independent alpha classes established in financial alpha mining research. In the second stage, the framework adopts a multimodal, multi-agent decision-making process. This multi-agent approach allows for the integration of different risk perspectives, enhancing the strategy's adaptability and robustness under various market conditions. The final stage involves a weight optimization method using deep learning techniques to evaluate the performance of each seed alpha and construct the overall strategy. The framework is designed to be dynamic, allowing for continuous improvement and adaptation. As new research emerges and market conditions evolve, the Seed Alpha Factory can be incrementally updated by providing the LLM with additional information. This continuous update mechanism ensures the Seed Alpha Factory remains relevant and robust. Similarly, the multi-agent decision-making process can adapt to changing market conditions by selecting a new set of seed alphas, maintaining the strategy’s effectiveness.

## LLM Filtering and Classification

The first stage of the methodology involves the LLM filtering and classification process for alpha-related research. This stage utilizes LLMs’ summarization and classification capabilities to accelerate the understanding of recent alpha mining research and to build the Seed Alpha Factory. In this study, a custom version of ChatGPT, named “Alpha Grail,” is used to perform LLM filtering and classification tasks. Alpha Grail’s primary function is to assist quantitative researchers by processing alpha-related research documents to construct the Seed Alpha Factory. Through this process, Alpha Grail generated nine categories containing 100 seed alphas. The LLM filtering and classification stage also integrates multimodal processing capabilities. By combining text, images, tables, and charts from research documents, the LLM provides a more comprehensive analysis and classification of seed alphas. This multimodal approach enhances the LLM’s ability to capture details and relationships within the research, creating a more robust and diverse Seed Alpha Factory.

## Multimodal and Multi-agent Evaluation of Alpha Sets

The second stage of the methodology involves comprehensive evaluation and selection of alpha factors through multimodal and multi-agent systems. This method ensures diversified and thorough evaluation of seed alphas by integrating different data sources and utilizing multiple analytical perspectives. In this stage, we combined multimodal data, including text, numbers, visuals, and multimedia inputs, to enrich the evaluation process. This comprehensive approach allows for a nuanced understanding of market dynamics and alpha factor performance, enhancing the robustness of the evaluation process. The multi-agent system is composed of various agents with different risk preferences and investment strategies. These agents analyze multimodal data and evaluate seed alphas based on their respective criteria. Each agent conducts a risk preference analysis, evaluating seed alphas according to their predefined risk parameters. This ensures that the evaluation covers a wide range of risk perspectives. Agents then select alpha factors that align with their risk preferences and market analysis, ensuring relevance and robustness under different market scenarios. To enhance the reliability of the selected alpha factors, a confidence scoring mechanism is implemented. Each agent assigns a confidence score to the alpha factors based on their analysis and historical performance, reflecting the agent’s assessment of the alpha factor’s predictive power and robustness. The selected alpha factors undergo rigorous backtesting, using historical market data to evaluate their performance under various market conditions. This step is crucial in identifying the most promising alpha factors for the final strategy.

## Optimizing Weights for Alpha Strategies

To optimize the weights of the selected seed alphas, we employ deep neural networks (DNNs) to fit the alpha values with future returns. The network architecture includes input, hidden, and output layers. The input layer uses daily alpha calculations derived from historical closing prices as input features. The hidden layer is equipped with ten nodes and uses a ReLU activation function to introduce non-linearity, enhancing the model’s learning capabilities. The output layer consists of a single node that predicts future returns. During the training phase, the network uses backpropagation and gradient descent to minimize a loss function, which quantifies the difference between the predicted and actual returns. This iterative process adjusts the network’s weights and biases to improve predictive accuracy. To ensure the model's generalization ability and prevent overfitting, a separate validation set is used. This approach establishes a robust framework for predicting future returns based on historical alpha values, forming an effective investment strategy. The DNN model processes the input data through the hidden layers, transforming them through learned weights and biases. The final output is generated by applying another set of weights, biases, and activation functions at the output layer. Our approach demonstrates the effectiveness of leveraging deep learning techniques to optimize alpha weights, improving the predictive performance of investment strategies.

# Related Work

In the field of quantitative investment, formulaic alpha refers to a series of systematic, rule-based strategies aimed at achieving excess returns by identifying and exploiting specific patterns or anomalies in the market. These strategies are typically based on predefined mathematical models or algorithms and executed through automated trading systems. Genetic programming is a common method for creating formulaic alphas, generating new alpha strategies through structural and numerical mutations. Additionally, machine learning techniques, particularly neural networks such as Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks and Transformer models, have gained attention for their ability to capture complex temporal dependencies in time series data. Decision tree models, such as XGBoost and LightGBM, are favored for their interpretability and ability to handle high-dimensional data. Explainable AI (XAI) methods and tools enhance investor trust and understanding by providing insights into model decision-making processes.

Large language models in the financial domain (Fin-LLMs) are specialized models designed for financial tasks, demonstrating potential in handling financial data and executing financial tasks. Although general-domain large language models (LLMs) have made significant progress, Fin-LLMs are still evolving. Open-source LLMs, such as LLaMA, BLOOM, and Flan-T5, offer flexibility and privacy on self-hosted servers but may not perform as well on financial tasks compared to proprietary models. Fin-LLMs fine-tuned for financial tasks perform better on financial tasks, showing enhanced understanding of domain-specific knowledge. However, their performance in generative tasks may be comparable to or slightly lower than general LLMs, indicating a need for higher-quality domain-specific datasets to further improve performance.

The application of multimodal and multi-agent LLMs in the investment domain demonstrates their potential in processing and integrating various types of data. These models can analyze news articles, social media posts, trading charts, and other data sources, providing more comprehensive market insights. Through multimodal analysis, models can more accurately predict market trends and investor sentiment. In portfolio management, multimodal LLMs can offer personalized investment recommendations based on individual investor risk preferences and financial goals. Additionally, these models can automate routine tasks such as data input and report generation, allowing investment professionals to focus on more strategic decisions. Multi-agent systems, by simulating different market scenarios and stress-testing strategies, help fund managers develop robust strategies that can withstand market fluctuations. The collaborative operation of such systems ensures the parallel testing and optimization of multiple strategies, facilitating a balanced consideration of risk and return.

Despite the promising applications of multimodal and multi-agent LLMs in finance, their implementation raises ethical and governance considerations. Ensuring transparency, accountability, and adherence to ethical guidelines and regulatory standards is crucial for maintaining investor trust and market integrity. Moreover, managing potential biases in data and model training is key to preventing systemic risks and ensuring fair outcomes.

This chapter also discusses the potential benefits of applying expert mixture (MoE) structures and financial knowledge graph technologies to strategy development. MoE structures enhance learning efficiency and model performance by dynamically allocating computational resources to different “experts.” This approach helps the model learn and distinguish features of different categories of seed alphas more effectively. Financial knowledge graph technologies, by introducing the complexity and interconnectivity of financial markets, contribute to better performance during the stock selection phase. The integration of these technologies offers new possibilities for the development of quantitative investment strategies, potentially improving model accuracy and market adaptability.

## 优化权重的Alpha策略
为了优化选定种子alpha的权重，我们采用深度神经网络（DNN）来拟合alpha值与未来收益。网络架构包括输入层、隐藏层和输出层。输入层采用历史收盘价派生的每日alpha计算作为输入特征。隐藏层配备十个节点，使用ReLU激活函数引入非线性，增强模型的学习能力。输出层由一个节点组成，预测未来收益。在训练阶段，网络采用反向传播和梯度下降来最小化损失函数，该函数量化预测和实际收益之间的差异。这一迭代过程调整网络的权重和偏差，以提高预测准确性。为确保模型的泛化能力并防止过拟合，使用单独的验证集。这种方法建立了一个基于历史alpha值预测未来收益的强大框架，从而形成有效的投资策略。DNN模型通过隐藏层处理输入数据，通过学习到的权重和偏差进行转换。最终输出是通过在输出层应用另一组权重、偏差和激活函数生成的。我们的方法展示了利用深度学习技术优化alpha权重的有效性，从而提高投资策略的预测性能。

本章旨在开发一个全面的由大型语言模型（LLM）驱动的alpha挖掘框架，该框架无需人工干预即可运行。该框架能够处理多模态信息并适应不同的市场条件。系统中的一个关键创新是引入了置信度评分，旨在减少LLM在种子alpha选择过程中可能出现的错误信息。通过将种子alpha回测结果与多模态市场条件整合，框架能够更全面地理解市场动态，以及每个类别种子alpha相关的情绪。研究的主要目标包括评估框架的有效性、多模态知识提取能力、置信度评分机制的有效性，以及整体策略的表现。为了验证框架的有效性，进行了一系列实验，以回答以下研究问题（RQs）：

## 数据集
研究聚焦于中国A股市场的金融数据，特别是针对上证50指数。数据集包括六个主要特征作为alpha因子的原始输入：开盘、收盘、最高、最低、成交量和成交量加权平均价格（VWAP）。为了确保严格的评估和稳健的模型性能，数据集被划分为不同的时间段：训练集从2021年1月1日到2022年12月31日，测试集从2023年1月1日到2023年12月31日。实验整合了上证50指数成分股公司的财务报告和因子表现，提供了市场的全面视图。

# 相关工作

在量化投资领域，公式化alpha指的是一系列系统性、基于规则的策略，旨在通过识别和利用市场的特定模式或异常来实现超额回报。这些策略通常基于预定义的数学模型或算法，并通过自动化交易系统执行。遗传编程是创造公式化alpha的常见方法，它通过结构和数值的变异来生成新的alpha策略。此外，机器学习技术，尤其是神经网络如长短期记忆网络（LSTM）和Transformer模型，因其在捕捉时间序列数据中的复杂时序依赖关系方面的能力而受到重视。决策树模型，如XGBoost和LightGBM，因其可解释性和处理高维数据的能力而受到青睐。可解释人工智能（XAI）方法和工具通过提供对模型决策过程的深入洞察，增强了投资者对模型的信任和理解。

金融领域大型语言模型（Fin-LLM）是专门为金融任务设计的模型，它们在处理金融数据和执行金融任务方面展现出了潜力。尽管通用领域大型语言模型（LLM）已经取得了显著进展，但金融领域的LLM仍在不断发展中。开源LLM，如LLaMA、BLOOM和Flan-T5，提供了在自托管服务器上的灵活性和隐私保护，但在金融任务上可能不如专有模型表现出色。针对金融任务进行微调的LLM在金融领域的任务上表现更佳，显示出对金融领域特定知识的增强理解。然而，在生成任务中，这些模型的性能可能与通用LLM相当或略逊一筹，这表明需要更高质量的领域特定数据集来进一步提升性能。

多模态与多智能体LLM在投资领域的应用，展示了它们在处理和整合多种类型数据方面的潜力。这些模型能够分析新闻文章、社交媒体帖子、交易图表等多种数据源，从而提供更全面的市场洞察。通过这种多模态分析，模型能够更准确地预测市场动向和投资者情绪。在投资组合管理方面，多模态LLM能够根据个人投资者的风险偏好和财务目标提供个性化的投资建议。此外，这些模型还能够自动化执行数据输入、报告生成等常规任务，使投资专业人士能够将注意力集中在更具战略意义的决策上。多智能体系统通过模拟不同的市场情景和压力测试，帮助基金经理制定出能够抵御市场波动的稳健策略。这种系统的协作操作确保了可以并行测试和优化多种策略，促进了对风险和回报的平衡考虑。

尽管多模态与多智能体LLM在金融领域的应用前景广阔，但它们的实施也引发了伦理和治理方面的考虑。确保透明度、问责制以及遵守道德指导方针和监管标准对于维护投资者信任和市场完整性至关重要。此外，管理数据和模型训练中的潜在偏见也是防止系统性风险和确保公平结果的关键。

本章还讨论了将专家混合（MoE）结构和金融知识图谱技术应用于策略开发的潜在好处。MoE结构通过动态分配计算资源给不同的“专家”，提高了学习效率和模型性能。这种方法有助于模型更有效地学习和区分不同类别的种子alpha特征。金融知识图谱技术通过引入金融市场的复杂性和互联性，有助于在股票选择阶段实现更好的性能。这些技术的融合为量化投资策略的开发提供了新的可能性，有望进一步提升模型的预测准确性和市场适应性。

本文概述了研究的主要贡献和成果，提出了一个创新的框架，该框架利用大型语言模型（LLMs）和多智能体架构在量化股票投资和投资组合管理中挖掘和优化alpha因子。通过在中国股市的广泛实验，证明了该框架在多个金融指标上显著优于现有的基准和传统方法。此外，本章还强调了结合LLM生成的alpha和多智能体架构以实现卓越交易性能和稳定性的潜力，并为未来研究提供了方向，包括探索将多智能体架构转变为专家混合（MoE）结构的可能性，以及将金融知识图谱技术整合到策略开发过程中，以进一步提升模型性能和市场预测的准确性。
